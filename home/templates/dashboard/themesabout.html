Land Acknowledgement

Indigenous people have migrated and cared for this land and water for millennia. I’d also like to take a moment to pay respect to the African Ancestors, to recognize the legacy of the kidnapping and the enslavement of African People, who never surrendered and who built the infrastructure of this country. Acknowledgements are nowhere near enough. I wanted to make space for this in my talk as one way for us settler allies remember the truth of this history and honor the contributions and traditions of Indigenous people is one way to reflect on our participation in settler colonialism.

Who is the site for

Artists and Creative Laborers can find jobs, gigs, collaborators, friends, and mentors. Community Organizations and Arts and Cultural Institutions can find artists, audiences, community-based collaborators, and each other. Art Lovers, Neighborhood Stakeholders and the Curious can find new practices to relish, creative neighbors, and contemporary Chicago-based art to see and support.

{% load static %}
{% load static %}

How to use the site

Casually: Anyone can check this site for cultural events, arts journalism, creative grant opportunities and more, without logging in. Professionally: Funders, galleries, venues, neighborhood arts organizations, and more are encouraged to use this site to connect their opportunities with arts professionals and audiences. Communally: By logging in, users can unlock powerful tools to share resources and collaborate. We encourage collaboration across disciplines, amongst institutions, and within and outside of narrow conceptions of the arts.
